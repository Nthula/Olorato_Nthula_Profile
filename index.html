Fig. 02 — Background

About Me

I'm Olorato Nthula, an Information Systems graduate (Major: Computer Information Systems) from the University of Botswana, Class of 2025. My path into technology started with a simple curiosity: how do organisations actually use information to make better decisions? That question has shaped everything since — from the modules I chose, to the way I approach every project.

During my industrial attachment at the Bank of Botswana, I worked alongside the IT support team — setting up equipment, resolving day-to-day technical issues for staff, supporting event and presentation systems, and helping keep records and systems running smoothly. It gave me a grounded, practical understanding of how technology supports a real business environment, not just a classroom one.

What I enjoy most is the analytical side of the work: gathering requirements, mapping out processes, working with data in Excel, SQL and Power BI, and translating findings into something a decision-maker can actually act on. I see myself as a bridge — someone who can sit in a meeting with business stakeholders and just as comfortably sit with a dataset or a database schema.

Mission

To help organisations make sense of their data and systems — and to keep learning, one project at a time, as technology and business needs evolve together.
